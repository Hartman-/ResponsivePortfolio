APPLICATIONS

Houdini

Houdini is my software package of choice, covering all aspects of the pipeline it gives me great flexibility to concept, develop, and deliver projects.

  • Created a variety of effects across multiple shots, inluduing fire/smoke, dust, a fractured planetary core, rocket engines, and VDB clouds to name a few.
  • Learned Houdini during off-hours throughout the past year to gain a level of comfort before going into my capstone project.

Usually NukeX.

  • Bash comping new fx renders to compositing the final shot, I rarely don't have Nuke open.
  • Developed a script to create burn-ins for dailies as well as a command line script generator.

The backbone of my team's capstone project, Maya was the obvious choice to ground the project in for asset creation and scene construction.

  • Integration of pipeline tools built with Python.
  • Modeling, Rigging, Animation, Shading, Lighting, FX, and Rendering... Experienced the full breadth of the pipeline in one program.

Cleaned and rebuilt assets from AutoCad and Revit to be used for final renderings. Often jumped between jobs to lend support where it was needed.

  • Multiple roles from scene building to shader development.
  • Developed shot environments to give key buildings context.

Current render engine of choice, just edging out Mantra... I can use both in Houdini. Effective use of RIB archives kept scene generation times low and the pipeline flexible.

  • Debugging render errors and optimizing scenes to get the most of out the engine.
  • Custom Renderman workspace to give artist's piece of mind knowing their renders will work locally or on the renderfarm.

I was part of a small team at Brooklyn Digital Foundry that specialized in Architectural Visualization where our primary render engine was V-ray. V-ray offered us the ability to develop realistic shaders and lighting at a speed that kept the render farm busy and allowed for quick iteration.

  • Used with both Maya and 3ds Max for multiple projects, mainly Architectural Visualization work.
  • Learned on my own time early second year as the curriculm at the time wasn't teaching students Vray.

Python is the backbone of my work to make my process more efficient. Writing small tools that shave time or that automate the small tasks make the world of difference. As well as building a launcher that enables my team's artist to quickly launch into shots and not have to worry about the small things like file naming conventions or even just giving Maya a more proper iterate and save.

  • Sequence renaming to launcher/pipeline management, Python serves a variety of roles in my everyday work.
  • Use of PyQT/Pyside for GUI for their native integration into current software packages. Easy to write code for one application and transfer to another.
